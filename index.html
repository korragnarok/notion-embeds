Notes on Delivery

  • Rage bait: say it completely straight, zero smile, stare at camera after, then pivot immediately
  • Outrageous/chaos hooks: deadpan delivery is everything. The second you smirk it loses power.
  • Husband reaction hooks: warm and conversational, like you're texting a friend
  • Tall girl hooks: matter of fact, like you've had this conversation a hundred times

Advanced techniques

01
Mirror Reflection Reveal
Film yourself walking up to a mirror — the product or message is written on the mirror in dry-erase or lip liner. Camera only sees the reflection until you're right in front of it.
camera angle
02
Polaroid Shake to Life
Hold a printed polaroid (or a phone playing video) up to the camera — shake it — and it "develops" into the current scene you're standing in. Cut on action.
transition
03
Reading Your Own Caption
Start the video looking down, then look up and say the exact text that appears on screen as your caption — like the video just became self-aware.
on-screen text
04
Two-Sided Whiteboard Flip
Hold a whiteboard/clipboard facing away from camera. Flip it around to reveal a message, drawing, or product — then flip back and it's different.
prop
05
Walking Through a Doorway = Scene Change
Walk through a doorframe with one outfit/location, but cut mid-step as the foot crosses the threshold — you exit into a totally different environment.
transition
06
Pinch and Zoom on Yourself
Make a "zoom out" pinch gesture at the camera — and the frame actually zooms out to reveal you're standing somewhere surprising, or holding something unexpected.
transitioncamera angle
07
Phone Screen Reflection Catch
Start recording with your phone angled so the screen reflects back into camera — for a split second the viewer sees you seeing yourself. Disorienting and stops the scroll.
POV
08
Scribble Out the Text in Real Time
Start with text on screen, then reach up with a marker (or your finger) and physically scribble it out — replaced by new text. Done in post with basic editing but looks seamless.
on-screen text
09
Voice Memo Playback as Hook
Hold up your phone showing the Voice Memos app playing. The "waveform" animates as your actual audio plays — viewer sees and hears at once. Raw + real.
prop
10
Camera on the Ceiling Looking Straight Down
Mount or hold the phone flat against the ceiling. Overhead top-down shot of you on a bed, floor, or desk. Immediately unusual. Talk to camera from below like a conversation with the sky.
camera angle
11
Unfolding a Torn Piece of Paper
Open on a crumpled piece of paper being unfolded toward the camera. The message or hook line is handwritten on it. Feels urgent and personal — like a note passed in class.
prop
12
Freeze Frame + Walk Back In
Video pauses mid-action and a caption floats in. Then it un-pauses and you keep going — like someone hit pause on your life to add commentary.
on-screen text
13
Waking Up and Rolling Over to Face Camera
Start the video lying on a pillow — eyes closed. Then open your eyes and look directly at the camera. Immediately intimate and catches people off guard.
POV
14
Window Reflection Double Exposure
Stand facing a dark window at night. Your reflection overlaps with the outside scene. Talk to your own reflection. Visually layered and eerie in a compelling way.
camera anglePOV
15
Slo-Mo Product Drop (Zero Words)
Open with no talking — just a slow-motion drop of the product into frame from above. It lands. Caption appears. Only then do you come on camera. The silence + motion = scroll stopper.
propcamera angle
16
Hand Slides Out a Recipe Card / Index Card
A hand slowly slides a physical index card into frame — on it is a handwritten "3 things I wish I knew before buying X." Analog in a digital feed = stops thumbs.
prop
17
Two Phones / Split Reality
Hold a second phone up to the camera showing a different scene (a before, a different location, a "what they show vs reality"). Creates an instant visual contrast in one shot.
prop
18
Clock or Timer as the Hook
Hold your phone showing a visible countdown timer (30 seconds). Start talking against the clock. The ticking urgency keeps people watching even if they weren't planning to.
prop
19
Talking Through a Shower Door / Frosted Glass
Film from outside a frosted glass shower door, bathroom window, or translucent panel. Silhouette visible but blurred. Voice comes through clearly. Mysterious and unexpected.
camera angle
20
Smash Cut to Black + One Word
Start mid-sentence, mid-action — then HARD CUT to black with one word in white text for 1 second, then back to you continuing. Feels like a film trailer. Uncommonly used in UGC.
on-screen texttransition
